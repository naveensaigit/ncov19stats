▶   The SIRD Model is a Mathematical Model used to predict outbreaks/pandemics. It assumes that people are of 4 categories -
  • Susceptible - People who are unaffected by the disease and could get it in the future.
  • Infected - People who have got the disease and could potentially spread it to others.
  • Recovered - People who were infected but have recovered. (It is assumed that these people are no longer susceptible)
  • Dead - People who were infected and died due to the disease.
▶  The model has been used widely around the world to analyse the spread of COVID-19. I have used this model to predict the number of cases in India.

▶  To know more about this model, head to the About Section

▶  The Predictions section contains comparison of Predicted vs Actual no.s of last 10 days and predictions for the next 10 days as well. A cumulative plot of predicted and actual cases is also present to give an idea of the accuracy of the model. Click on the legend of the plot to view individual plots.

▶  The Long Term Prediction section contains the probable total no. of cases after 1 year or so. This could vary depending on how fast a cure is developed.
